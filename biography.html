Biography

Full Name: Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam
Born: October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
Died: July 27, 2015, Shillong, Meghalaya, India
Occupation: Aerospace Scientist, Author, President of India (2002–2007)
Known As: “Missile Man of India”
Awards: Bharat Ratna, Padma Vibhushan, Padma Bhushan

“Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action.”

Career

Dr. Kalam was a pioneer in aerospace science and leadership. He worked with ISRO and DRDO, contributed to SLV and missile projects, and served as the 11th President of India from 2002–2007.

He passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong in 2015.

Achievements

Some of his major achievements include:
- Development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III)
- Key roles in Agni and Prithvi missile programs
- Pokhran-II nuclear tests (1998)
- Honored with Bharat Ratna and other top civilian awards

Goals

Dr. Kalam envisioned India as a developed nation by 2020, focusing on education, innovation, and youth empowerment.

“You have to dream before your dreams can come true.”
